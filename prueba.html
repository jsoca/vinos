History


MW was created on March 7 , 1916 , through the merger of two Munich mechanical companies, the Bayerische Flugzeugwerke, and Rapp Motorenwerke , a manufacturer of aircraft engines. The BMW brand emblem takes up the black circle of the Rapp logo, with blue and white in the center symbolizing the colors of Bavaria .

Series 1 to 8


BMW 325 XI.
Since the 1970s , the brand's models have been identified by a three-digit number, the first corresponding to the vehicle category, the other two to the engine displacement (with exceptions) followed by a letter indicating the fuel used or the type of transmission.
The current main series are:
BMW 1 Series : Compact city car , available in three or five-door versions. Two generations since 2004, E87 and F20 (2011);
BMW 2 Series : coupe and convertible. There is also an active tourer and gran tourer variant (minivan, respectively five and seven seats). A generation since 2014 and a minivan, F22 and F45.
BMW 3 Series : family sedan, also available in station wagon ( touring ) and in Gran Turismo (GT). Seven generations since 1975, E21 , E30 (1982), E36 (1990), E46 (1998), E90-93 (2005), F30 (2012) and G20 (2019).
BMW 4 Series : coupé, coupé-cabriolet and four-door coupé (GranCoupé). A generation since 2013, F32. BMW 5 Series : luxury sedan, also available in station wagon and GT ( Gran Tourismo ). Seven generations since 1972, E12 , E28 (1981), E34 (1988), E39 (1995), E60-61 (2003), F10-11 (2010) and G30-31 (2016).
BMW 6 Series : coupé, four-door GranCoupé, convertible and in GT (Gran Tourismo). Three generations since 1976, E24, E63-64 (2004), F12-13-06 (2011 to 2018) and G32 (2018)
BMW 7 Series : prestige sedan, high-end. Five generations since 1977, E23, E32 (1986), E38 (1994), E65-68 (2001), F01-02 (2008) and G11-12 (2015).
BMW 8 Series : coupé, Cabriolet and four-door GranCoupé. Two generations since 1989, E31 and G14-15 (planned for 2018).
